## Planning

- Chart out your meals in advance \> make a list for groceries
- Use only food grade plastic/materials for storage
- Combine foods for taste and nutrition
- Packages saying feeding 2 people means 1

### Easy Homemade Energy Bars

[Original source - no longer
available](http://www.coach.ca/faites-le-plein-d-nergie-sur-le-terrain-de-soccer--p154662&language=en)
From: Dufour, A: PowerFUEL Food (October, 2012)

#### Ingredients

- 1 ¼ cups water
- 3 tbsp corn syrup
- 4 lb low fat oatmeal muffin mix
- 1 cup raisins (or dried cranberries, chopped dates, apricots, etc.)
- ⅓ cup sunflower seeds
- (or almonds)
- ½ cup unsweetened
- shredded coconut
- 1 tbsp cinnamon

#### Method

1.  Preheat oven to 325F. In a large bowl, mix together water, corn
    syrup, and muffin mix until water is absorbed.
2.  Stir in raisins, sunflower seeds and cinnamon.
3.  Place parchment paper on a cookie sheet and spread mixture evenly
    over paper.
4.  Add another piece of parchment paper on top of mixture and roll with
    rolling pin to 1” thick.
5.  Remove top sheet of paper and bake for 20-25 minutes or until golden
    brown.
6.  Cool in pan 45 minutes. Turn upside down and remove paper. Cut into
    32 bars.
