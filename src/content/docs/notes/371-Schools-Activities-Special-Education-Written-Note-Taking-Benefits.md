## Use of Written Notes

Even when a digital medium is available, written notes can help with
thinking about real world application of what you are learning. Notes
can summarize the use case for testing and review later.

Paper gives advantages with a digital medium like a computer or when one
is not present:

- Free form input and drawing of visuals
- Can complement using a device with the paper as an additional place to
  think, do visuals. Example: doing computer programming and debug and
  writing the call stack on paper, similar to rubber duck debugging.
- Paper notes are easy to review, scan, and randomly access on the go

## Workflow with Physical Written Notes and Digital Mediums

A workflow might be to use written notes when learning, review, and
record in a digital format important points like items for an exam and
things to remember in future.

Use the digital format to write que cards, for example use with a spaced
repetition system like Anki. Those cards and course material are useful
as reference.

``` text

Writing Notes --▶ Learning / Review / Scan, reflects point
       │
       │
       │  Transfer key points
       │  to remember
       ▼
Digital Notes / Cards ◀-----------------▶ Spaced Repetition System
                        edit and refine
                        during recall

```

## Supporting Research

Studies support writing notes helps with information recall and
comprehension:

[Take notes by hand for better long-term comprehension -
ScienceDaily](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2014/04/140424102837.htm)

Writing note takes compared to laptop note takers tended to do better
with conceptual understanding and recall. Possible reasons are time
constraints in writing, ability to take visuals, and needing to capture
concepts rather than transcription of learning.

[Study shows stronger brain activity after writing on paper than on
tablet or smartphone -
ScienceDaily](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2021/03/210319080820.htm)

Writing on physical paper leads to more brain activity helping with
recall. "Unique, complex, spatial, and tactile information" of writing
likely leads to improved memory. Non-standardization of notebooks and
their spatial complexity help with brain activity. Conclusion is use
paper notebooks for memorization and learning.
